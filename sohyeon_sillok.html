昭顯世子 · 仁祖大王實錄

Selected passages from the Annals of King Injo (仁祖大王實錄) concerning Crown Prince Sohyeon (昭顯世子, 1612–1645)

昭顯世子（1612–1645），名𪶁，字成之，朝鮮第十六代王仁祖之長子，元妃韓氏所出。仁祖三年（1625）冊立為王世子，時年十四歲。仁祖十五年（1637）丙子胡亂後，作為人質赴清朝瀋陽，滯留長達八年。仁祖二十三年（1645）春歸國，數月後薨逝於昌慶宮，年三十四，謚曰昭顯。

Note: During his lifetime, the Sillok refers to the Crown Prince as 王世子 or 世子. The posthumous title 昭顯 was conferred after his death in the fifth month of 仁祖 23年.

仁祖三年 · 1625  ｜  冊封
Investiture as Crown Prince
仁祖三年 正月 二十一日
上御隆政殿，命慶昌君珘爲主，右議政申欽爲賓，會百官宣敎，行元子冠禮於景賢堂。
仁祖三年 正月 二十七日
上御隆政殿，受群臣賀，蓋賀王世子冊禮成也。……命又授竹冊。元子𪶁，時年十四，冊立爲王世子。
仁祖五年 · 1627  ｜  丁卯胡亂
The First Manchu Invasion: court debates sending the Crown Prince south
仁祖五年 正月 十八日
世子年幼，不可遠去矣。
仁祖五年 正月 二十一日
世子雖幼沖，若向南方，則人心有所依賴矣。
Officials argued that even a young Crown Prince sent south could serve as a focal point of legitimacy for a divided court (分朝).
仁祖五年 正月 二十四日
世子發行。……世子至全州。
仁祖十五年 · 1637  ｜  丙子胡亂 · 入質瀋陽
The Second Manchu Invasion: the surrender at Samjondo and departure for Shenyang
仁祖十五年 正月 三十日 · 三田渡
王世子從焉。
The Crown Prince accompanied King Injo to Samjondo (三田渡) for the surrender ceremony.
仁祖十五年 二月 一日
王世子及嬪宮、鳳林大君及夫人於陣中。……以麟坪大君及夫人，還送于京中。
The Crown Prince, his consort, and 鳳林大君 were detained in the Qing camp. 麟坪大君 was sent back to the capital.
仁祖十五年 二月 五日
世子自虜營返回，向群臣辭別。……上敎：「說書兪棨在山城，極言和議之非……命勿陪往。」
仁祖十五年 二月 八日 · 昌陵 西
上於昌陵西送別世子。世子臨別受敎：「勉之哉！勿激怒，勿見輕。」世子對群臣曰：「主上在此，何敢乃爾？」
King Injo's parting words to the Crown Prince: "Do your best. Do not provoke anger, and do not let yourself be looked down upon." The Crown Prince replied to the officials: "The King is here — how could any of you dare act otherwise?"
仁祖十六年 · 1638  ｜  在瀋陽
Life in Shenyang as a hostage
仁祖十六年 三月 二十一日
世子在瀋陽，平安監司每朔進膳。世子念農務方緊，令減四月朔膳，西民感悅。
The Pyeongan provincial governor sent monthly provisions to the Crown Prince in Shenyang. Knowing the farming season was pressing, the Crown Prince ordered the April provisions reduced, which earned goodwill from the western population.
仁祖十六年 四月 二十五日 · 端午
王世子遣輔德李時楷問安，以端午節日也。
On the Dragon Boat Festival, the Crown Prince dispatched his attendant 李時楷 back to Korea to inquire after the King's health.
仁祖十六年 五月 二十日
世子有疾，遣宣傳官于瀋陽以問之。
仁祖二十三年 · 1645  ｜  歸國 · 薨逝 · 賜謚
Return from Shenyang, sudden death at Changgyeong Palace, and bestowal of the posthumous title 昭顯
仁祖二十三年 二月 十八日
世子還，淸使偕入京。先是，淸使固要上郊迎……於是，上扶掖而出，迎于闕庭。
The Crown Prince returned to the capital accompanied by Qing envoys. The Qing envoys had insisted that King Injo personally go out to the suburbs to receive his son; ultimately the King went out and welcomed him in the palace courtyard.
仁祖二十三年 四月 二十六日
王世子卒于昌慶宮歡慶堂。世子姿質英明，而器量不弘……世子十年異域，備嘗險艱，東還纔數月而遘疾，醫官等亦妄施鍼藥，終至不救。……世子年三十四，有三子三女。
The Crown Prince died suddenly at Hwangyeong Hall in Changgyeong Palace. He was 34 years old. The Sillok notes that after ten years in a foreign land he had endured great hardship, and that the royal physicians' improper use of acupuncture and medicine contributed to his death.
仁祖二十三年 五月 十六日
議謚曰昭顯。
The posthumous title 昭顯 was officially determined.
仁祖二十四年 · 1646  ｜  嬪宮姜氏之獄
The trial and execution of the Crown Prince's consort, Consort Gang (愍懷嬪)
仁祖二十四年 正月 三日
上進生鰒灸有毒，遂疑姜嬪。
King Injo was served grilled abalone suspected of containing poison, and suspicion fell upon Consort Gang.
仁祖二十四年 二月 三日
備忘記云：「姜嬪在瀋之時，潛圖易位，預造紅錦翟衣，僭稱內殿之號。」
The royal memorandum accused Consort Gang of secretly plotting to usurp the queen's position while in Shenyang, having had royal robes made in advance and unlawfully used the title of the Inner Palace.
仁祖二十四年 二月 十二日
上下敎于政院：「廢出賜死之意，言于兩司。」
仁祖二十四年 三月 十四日
廢昭顯世子嬪姜氏，賜死於故第，收其敎命竹冊、印、章服焚之。
Consort Gang was stripped of her title and ordered to die at her former residence. Her investiture documents, bamboo scroll, seal, and ceremonial garments were confiscated and burned.
仁祖二十四年 五月 二日
命以姜氏私藏銀一萬六百五十兩、黃金一百六十兩、倭劍十九柄，歸于戶曹，使之別置一庫，以備昭顯諸子婚娶之需。
Consort Gang's private holdings — 10,650 taels of silver, 160 taels of gold, and 19 Japanese swords — were transferred to the Board of Revenue, to be held in a separate repository for the future marriages of 昭顯世子's sons.
仁祖二十五年 · 1647  ｜  三子流配濟州
The three sons of 昭顯世子 exiled to Jeju Island
仁祖二十五年 二月 四日
昭顯世子魂宮，姑設於仁慶宮內，而此非神主永妥之所。三年後似當依順懷宮之例，別立廟宇于他處。
The Ministry of Rites noted that the spirit shrine for 昭顯世子, temporarily housed at Ingyeong Palace, was not a suitable permanent location, and recommended following the precedent of 順懷宮 by establishing a separate shrine after three years.
仁祖二十五年 五月 十三日
配昭顯世子三子石鐵、石麟、石堅于濟州。上諭：「一處定配，使之相依以生。內官、別將等輪定以送。」
The three sons of 昭顯世子 — 石鐵 (age 12), 石麟 (age 8), and 石堅 (age 4) — were exiled to Jeju Island. King Injo directed that they be assigned to the same place so that they might support one another.
附錄  ｜  傳記 摘要
Biographical summary from the Appendix of 仁祖大王實錄
王元妃韓氏誕三男，長昭顯世子諱𪶁早卒，次今上殿下，次麟坪大君㴭。……丁丑往瀋陽，轉入燕京，乙酉春大歸，尋疾革殂逝。昭顯世子有三男、三女，男二死，一幼，三女未筓。
From the 附錄 (Appendix) of the 仁祖大王實錄: Queen Han bore three sons — 昭顯世子 (died young), the present King [Hyojong], and 麟坪大君. Sohyeon went to Shenyang in the 丁丑 year, later transferred to Beijing, returned in the spring of 乙酉 [1645], and soon died of illness. He had three sons and three daughters; two sons died, one was young, and three daughters were unmarried at his death.
